# XTERRA WHISTLER TRAINING 2023
Wednesday, August 09, 2023 - 39 days out

Sleep **3** | Fatigue **4** | Stress **4** | Soreness **4**  
<sup>Rate on an scale 1-7 &nbsp; 1=best 7=worst &nbsp; +5 is a warning</sup>

Weather: ☀️ 23&#8239;°C Gusty west wind   
Weight: **77.0&#8239;Kg**

### WORKOUT 1 &mdash; BIKE
MTB ~ 90 minutes. 
Easy effort. Head to the trails. Get on some single track. 
Have fun. You can push to get up the steep parts of trail, but 
generally speaking the goal is fun.

Time: **2:24:16** &nbsp; Distance: **62&#8239;km**
### NOTES
MTB is scheduled for service on Friday afternoon and I had an 
errand to run up in _Sidney_; TT bike for the win.

Swirling wind took me to my brother-in-law's house in 1:03:00. 
Thought I'd be in for a big headwind south but _John Dean_ and 
_Mount Work_ provided a wind shadow for the trip south.
<!----->
First time riding _Thompson Place_; nice hill.

A paceline of 4 roadies blew by me at the _Observatory_; I gave 
chase. They slowed for a re-group at the northeend of 
_Interurban_ and I passed them there.

That lead did not last long as 3 of 4 blew by me near _Quayle 
Road_. I pulled the 4th back up to his group near the _Camosun 
Campus_.  A red light at _Wilkinson Road_ allowed me to pass 
that crew once more.

I was super impressed at how quickly they could reel me in.

On the _Goose_, I stopped to assist one of that group unwedge 
his chain from between his frame and inner ring.  There I 
learned that the 3 speedsters were 21, 22 and 24 years old; 
all more than half my age (the 4th had already headed home).

As hopeless as it was to hold their wheel, I had a lot of 
fun trying; they had fun in effort too.
